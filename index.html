How this works: upload one audio recording per shloka (named so the filename includes the shloka number). Each recording is split automatically into segments — one per name — by detecting brief pauses in the chanting. Set "Start from shloka #" to wherever you want to begin (e.g. 4 or 40), then press Start. For each segment, the app plays it once (Listen), then gives you 3 timed pauses to repeat it aloud, then moves to the next segment — and on to the next shloka's audio once one is loaded. Use Pause/Resume to take a break, and Restart to go back to the beginning of the selected starting shloka.

Very short segments (under 0.6s, e.g. a standalone "Om") play once with no repeat pauses.

Hear yourself: on Start, the app asks for microphone access. During each "Your turn" pause it records you, then plays it back immediately afterward so you can hear your own chanting. If you decline microphone access, the session continues without playback.

Saved on this device: audio and text you upload are stored in this browser's local storage (IndexedDB) so they're available next time, without re-uploading. They are not uploaded anywhere else.

Note on segmentation: silence-based splitting is approximate — if a shloka splits into too many or too few segments, try adjusting the sensitivity slider and re-uploading that file.
